technology in the automotive industry

fuel efficiency

the first hybrid vehicle

  • in 1997, Toyota launched the Prius, the world's first mass produced hybrid car
  • the name is the Latin word for before, as the car was believed to be the predecessor for future vehicles
  • a 58hp engine was paired with a 40hp electric motor to reduce emissions and improve fuel economy
  • the Prius set the stage for a wave of more fuel efficient vehicles, which continues to this day

electric vehicles today

  • nowadays, electric cars can travel hundres of miles on a single charge
  • in 2016, more than 2 million electric vehicles were sold worldwide
  • they are also becoming more affordable - the Tesla Model 3 retails for just US$35k
  • electic vehicles, such as the BMW i3, are often built from sustainable materials in an attempt to promote a change in mindset of the automotive industry

the future of electric cars

  • Volvo has plegded to equip every engine they produce with an electric motor by 2019
  • it is expected that 20-25% of all vehicles sales in the US will be electric by 2030
  • by 2040, all cars sold in the UK will be hybrid or fully electric
  • Porsche recently announced that it wil stop making diesel vehicles and instead focus on petrol hybrid systems

autonomous driving

driver assistance systems

  • many cars, especially those manufactured by high-end companies, feature advanced driver aids
  • these systems utilise cutting-edge technology to reduce the strain on the driver
  • some example driver aids are
    • adaptive cruise control
    • lane departure assist
    • pedestrian collision warning

autonomous parking

  • as well as aids for use on the open road, many cars feature parking assistance systems
  • the majority of these use parking sensors to recognise parallel parking spaces
  • often, the driver doesn't even have to control the accelerator and brake during self-parking
  • some cars, such as the BMW 7 series, can be driven out of a garage using just the key
